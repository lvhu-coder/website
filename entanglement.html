Entanglement: Correlations Beyond Classical Limits

Quantum entanglement is a non-classical correlation between qubits where the state of one qubit instantly affects the state of another, regardless of distance. Einstein famously referred to it as “spooky action at a distance.”

Bell States

Entangled qubits are often represented using Bell states, such as:
|Φ+⟩ = (|00⟩ + |11⟩)/√2
The measurement of one qubit immediately determines the outcome of its partner, illustrating the strong correlations that are impossible in classical physics.

Applications

Entanglement is crucial for quantum teleportation, superdense coding, error correction codes, and enabling quantum algorithms to achieve speedups. Without entanglement, many advantages of quantum computing would not be possible.
